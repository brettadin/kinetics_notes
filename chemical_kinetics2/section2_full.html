2.0 Scope

All single-step integrated laws for 0th, 1st, 2nd (2A), and mixed (A+B) with unequal starts; linear tests and slope mappings; PFO and initial-rate workflows; dimensional checks; branching; and data‑reduction hygiene. Default axes: M and s.

2.1 Canonical orders (single‑reactant forms)

Zero order

Def.2.1.1 Rate independent of [A]. Saturation, photolysis at constant flux.

Eq.2.1.1 \( -\,\frac{d[A]}{dt}=k \) with \([k]=\mathrm{M\,s^{-1}}\).

Eq.2.1.2 \([A]_t=[A]_0-kt\).

Eq.2.1.3 \( t_{1/2}=[A]_0/(2k) \) depends on \([A]_0\).

First order

Def.2.1.2 Rate proportional to [A].

Eq.2.1.4 \( -\,\frac{d[A]}{dt}=k[A] \) with \([k]=\mathrm{s^{-1}}\).

Eq.2.1.5 \( \ln[A]_t=-kt+\ln[A]_0 \).

Eq.2.1.6 \( t_{1/2}=(\ln2)/k \) independent of \([A]_0\).

Eq.2.3.2 Fraction remaining: \( \dfrac{[A]_t}{[A]_0}=e^{-kt}=2^{-t/t_{1/2}} \).

Eq.2.3.3 Time to reach fraction \(f\): \( t=\dfrac{\ln(1/f)}{k} \).

Second order (2A→P)

Def.2.1.3 Bimolecular in a single species.

Eq.2.1.7 \( -\,\frac{d[A]}{dt}=k[A]^2 \) with \([k]=\mathrm{M^{-1}\,s^{-1}}\).

Eq.2.1.8 \( 1/[A]_t = kt + 1/[A]_0 \).

Half‑life \( t_{1/2}=1/(k[A]_0) \) depends strongly on \([A]_0\).

General m‑th order (advanced, single species)

Eq.2.3.1 For \(m\ne 1\): \( \dfrac{[A]_t^{\,1-m}-[A]_0^{\,1-m}}{1-m}=kt \). Reduces to Eq.2.1.5 when \(m\to1\).

Mixed second order (A+B→P)

With \(A_0\ne B_0\) and unit stoichiometry:

Eq.2.1.9 \[ \frac{1}{B_0-A_0}\,\ln\!\left(\frac{[B]_t A_0}{[A]_t B_0}\right)=kt \] Eq.2.1.10 Special case \(A_0=B_0\) reduces to Eq.2.1.8 with \([A]=[B]\).

2.2 Linear tests (pick what linearizes)

Def.2.2.1 A linear test ties a straight‑line plot to an integrated form; axes must match the algebra.

  • Eq.2.2.1 0th: \([A]\) vs \(t\), slope \(m=-k\) [M s\(^{-1}\)], intercept \([A]_0\).
  • Eq.2.2.2 1st: \(\ln[A]\) vs \(t\), slope \(m=-k\) [s\(^{-1}\)], intercept \(\ln[A]_0\).
  • Eq.2.2.3 2nd(2A): \(1/[A]\) vs \(t\), slope \(m=+k\) [M\(^{-1}\) s\(^{-1}\)], intercept \(1/[A]_0\).
  • Eq.2.2.4 Mixed 2nd(A+B): \(Y(t)\) vs \(t\) with \(Y(t)=\frac{1}{B_0-A_0}\ln\!\big(\frac{[B]_t A_0}{[A]_t B_0}\big)\), slope \(m=k\).
Regression hygiene. Report slope with units; interpret intercepts correctly; inspect residuals. Weighted fits help when early‑time points dominate error.

2.3 PFO and initial‑rate workflows

Pseudo‑first‑order (isolation)

Flood all but one reactant so their concentrations stay ~constant. Use Eq.1.2.3 to define \(k'\) [s\(^{-1}\)]; analyze as first order to get \(k'\), then back‑out \(k\).

Initial rates

Use Eq.1.2.4 with runs that vary only one reactant at a time. Log‑linear regression of \(\ln r_0\) vs \(\ln[A]_0,\ln[B]_0\) is robust.

2.4 Decision tree

  1. Try 0th: \([A]\) vs \(t\). Straight line? Use Eq.2.1.2.
  2. Else 1st: \(\ln[A]\) vs \(t\). Straight line? Use Eq.2.1.5.
  3. Else 2nd(2A): \(1/[A]\) vs \(t\). Straight line? Use Eq.2.1.8.
  4. If A+B with \(A_0\ne B_0\): compute \(Y(t)\) and plot vs \(t\); slope \(=k\).
  5. If none linearize well: invoke PFO or accept non‑elementary behavior.

2.5 Dimensional sanity

  • 0th \(k\): M s\(^{-1}\)
  • 1st \(k\): s\(^{-1}\)
  • 2nd \(k\): M\(^{-1}\) s\(^{-1}\)

2.6 Product distributions in parallel (branching)

For \(A\to B\) with \(k_B\) and \(A\to C\) with \(k_C\): \(k_{\text{tot}}=k_B+k_C\); yields \(Y_B=k_B/k_{\text{tot}}\); number ratio \(n_B/n_C=k_B/k_C\); \([A]_t=A_0 e^{-k_{\text{tot}} t}\).

2.7 From signal to [A] (lab reality)

  • Beer–Lambert: \(A=\varepsilon \ell [A]\). If you plot \(\ln A\) vs \(t\) you still get slope \(-k\) for first order (constant \(\varepsilon,\ell\)).
  • Emission/pressure proxies: document the calibration curve; propagate uncertainty into \(k\).
  • Check baseline drift and path‑length changes; they masquerade as kinetics.

2.8 Data‑reduction checklist

  • Use early‑time windows; avoid depletion artifacts for 0th and 2nd order.
  • Prefer linearized fits only when residuals are homoscedastic; otherwise fit the integrated form directly.
  • Show units on axes and on \(k\); quote \(k\pm\sigma\) if doing regression.

2.9 Worked‑example templates

  1. Order & \(k\) from time–[A]: pick test, regress, map slope→\(k\), verify with residuals, compute \(t_{1/2}\).
  2. Mixed 2nd with unequal starts: compute \(Y(t)\), slope \(=k\), cross‑check \([A]_t\) via back‑substitution.

2.10 Classic traps

  • Wrong linear test but reporting the slope as \(k\).
  • Using Eq.2.1.8 for A+B when \(A_0\ne B_0\).
  • Forgetting stoichiometric divisors in Eq.1.1.1 when inferring the overall rate from species slopes.
  • Quoting \(k\) with units that contradict the declared order.
