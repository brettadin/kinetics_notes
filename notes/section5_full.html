5.0 Catalysis in one breath

  • Def.5.0.1 Catalyst: lowers the barrier by offering an alternate path; appears in steps, not in the net reaction.
  • Def.5.0.2 Non‑negotiable: catalysts do not change \( \Delta G^\circ, \Delta H^\circ, K \). They change how fast, not where.
  • Def.5.0.3 Mechanistic reality: cycles introduce intermediates; observed orders shift with coverage, saturation, and inhibition.

5.1 Homogeneous catalysis: cycle logic and “effective” rate constants

Two‑step solution cycle: \(A+C \xrightleftharpoons[k_{-1}]{k_1} AC\) (fast), \(AC \xrightarrow{k_2} P+C\) (slow).

Pre‑equilibrium for step 1: \([AC]=K_1[A][C]\) with \(K_1=k_1/k_{-1}\); net rate \(r=k_2[AC]=k_2K_1[A][C]\).

Site/material balance when most catalyst is in a single resting state:

Eq.5.1.1 \( C_T=[C]+[AC] \Rightarrow [C]=\dfrac{C_T}{1+K_1[A]} \).

Effective rate law (saturation form): \( r=\dfrac{k_2K_1 C_T[A]}{1+K_1[A]} \). Low \([A]\): first order. High \([A]\): zero order.

5.2 Enzymes: Michaelis–Menten from SSA

Scheme: \(E+S \xrightleftharpoons[k_{-1}]{k_1} ES \xrightarrow{k_2} E+P\). Initial rate, substrate \(\gg\) enzyme, \(d[ES]/dt\approx 0\).

Eq.5.2.1 Michaelis–Menten: \( v\equiv r = \dfrac{V_{\max}[S]}{K_m+[S]} \).

Eq.5.2.2 Turnover & efficiency: \( k_{\mathrm{cat}}=V_{\max}/E_T\) [s\(^{-1}\)], \(k_{\mathrm{cat}}/K_m\) [M\(^{-1}\) s\(^{-1}\)]. Diffusion limit \( \sim 10^8\)–\(10^9\) M\(^{-1}\) s\(^{-1}\).

Linearizations (chalkboard‑use only; fit nonlinearly in practice)

  • Eq.5.2.3 Lineweaver–Burk: \( \dfrac{1}{v}=\dfrac{K_m}{V_{\max}}\dfrac{1}{[S]}+\dfrac{1}{V_{\max}} \). Slope \(=K_m/V_{\max}\); intercept \(=1/V_{\max}\).
  • Eq.5.2.4 Eadie–Hofstee: \( v=-K_m\,(v/[S]) + V_{\max} \). Slope \(=-K_m\); intercept \(=V_{\max}\).
  • Eq.5.2.5 Hanes–Woolf: \( [S]/v = [S]/V_{\max} + K_m/V_{\max} \). Slope \(=1/V_{\max}\); intercept \(=K_m/V_{\max}\).
Limits you must spot: Low \([S]\ll K_m\Rightarrow v\approx (V_{\max}/K_m)[S]\) (first order). High \([S]\gg K_m\Rightarrow v\approx V_{\max}\) (zero order).

5.3 Enzyme inhibition (minimal lies)

Define modifiers: \( \alpha=1+[I]/K_i \), \( \alpha'=1+[I]/K_i' \).

  • Competitive (I binds E): \( v=\dfrac{V_{\max}[S]}{\alpha K_m+[S]} \). LB lines share y‑intercept; slope increases. \(K_m\to \alpha K_m\); \(V_{\max}\) unchanged.
  • Uncompetitive (I binds ES only): \( v=\dfrac{V_{\max}[S]}{K_m+\alpha'[S]} \). LB lines parallel. \(K_m\to K_m/\alpha'\), \(V_{\max}\to V_{\max}/\alpha'\).
  • Pure noncompetitive (I binds E and ES equally, \(K_i=K_i'\)): \( v=\dfrac{(V_{\max}/\alpha')[S]}{K_m+[S]} \). LB lines share x‑intercept. \(K_m\) unchanged; \(V_{\max}\) down.
  • Mixed (general \(K_i\ne K_i'\)): \( v=\dfrac{(V_{\max}/\alpha')[S]}{(K_m/\alpha)+[S]} \). Both shift; LB lines meet in quadrant II.
  • Substrate inhibition: \( v=\dfrac{V_{\max}[S]}{K_m+[S]+[S]^2/K_i^{(S)}} \). Peak then decline at high \([S]\).

5.4 Heterogeneous catalysis (surfaces): Langmuir–Hinshelwood and friends

Coverage symbols: \(\theta\) fractional coverage, \(\theta_*\) empty sites fraction.

Eq.5.4.1 Langmuir isotherm for A on uniform sites: \( \theta_A=\dfrac{K_A P_A}{1+K_A P_A + K_B P_B + \cdots} \). Site balance: \( \theta_*+\sum\theta_i=1\).

Eq.5.4.2 Langmuir–Hinshelwood (A* + B* → P*): \( r = k\,\theta_A\,\theta_B = \dfrac{k K_A K_B P_A P_B}{(1+K_A P_A + K_B P_B)^2} \).

Eq.5.4.3 Eley–Rideal (A_g + B* → P*): \( r= k\,\theta_B\,P_A = \dfrac{k K_B P_B P_A}{1+K_B P_B} \).

Diagnosis: ER shows saturation in the adsorbing partner only; LH can show zero order or inhibition in either reactant at high coverage. Sabatier principle explains the volcano plots.

Units: surface rates often \(\mathrm{mol\,m^{-2}\,s^{-1}}\) or \(\mathrm{mol\,kg_{cat}^{-1}\,s^{-1}}\). If converted to volumetric M s\(^{-1}\), document surface area/dispersion assumptions.

5.5 Energy profiles and the “catalyst lowers \(E_a\)” line

Cycles yield several barriers and wells; the highest effective barrier on the dominant path sets the apparent rate. Catalysts change those heights, not the reactant–product \(\Delta G^\circ\). If your drawing moves the product well with a catalyst, it’s fiction.

5.6 Worked‑example blueprints (no numbers so you think)

  1. Extract \(K_m,V_{\max}\): Nonlinear fit to \(v=V_{\max}[S]/(K_m+[S])\); cross‑check with LB/Hanes; report \(k_{\mathrm{cat}}=V_{\max}/E_T\).
  2. Diagnose inhibition from LB family: same y‑intercept → competitive; parallel → uncompetitive; same x‑intercept → pure noncomp; else mixed (\(\alpha,\alpha'\)).
  3. LH vs ER on metal: measure order in \(P_A,P_B\) at low coverage; look for single‑partner saturation (ER) vs both (LH); fit to Eq.5.4.2 or 5.4.3 with units.

5.7 Micro‑review

  • Enzymes: \(v=V_{\max}[S]/(K_m+[S])\). Low \([S]\) first order, high \([S]\) zero order.
  • Inhibition signatures: same y‑intercept (competitive), parallel (uncompetitive), same x‑intercept (pure noncomp.).
  • Surfaces: LH requires two adsorptions; ER requires one.
