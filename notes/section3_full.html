3.0 What changes with temperature, exactly?

  • Def.3.0.1 Activation barrier \(E_a\): energy gap molecules must exceed along the minimum-energy path.
  • Def.3.0.2 Pre-exponential \(A\): everything non-exponential multiplying the Arrhenius exponential; bundles frequency, orientation, and degeneracy. Units of \(A\) match those of \(k\).
  • Def.3.0.3 Transition state \(^{\ddagger}\): the bottleneck configuration at the barrier top; not an isolable species.

3.1 Collision picture (intuition for Arrhenius)

Eq.3.1.1 qualitative collision form: \[ k \approx Z_{AB}\,p\,e^{-E_a/(RT)} \] \(Z_{AB}\): collision frequency per concentration unit; \(p\): steric factor (productive orientation fraction). The exponential is the fraction of encounters with sufficient energy.

3.2 Arrhenius equation and how to use it

Eq.3.2.1 \( k = A\,e^{-E_a/(RT)} \)

Units sanity: \([k]=\mathrm{M}^{1-n}\mathrm{s^{-1}}\), so \(A\) has identical units. \(E_a\) in J mol\(^{-1}\) (usually reported in kJ mol\(^{-1}\)); \(T\) in K; \(R=8.314\ \mathrm{J\,mol^{-1}\,K^{-1}}=0.008314\ \mathrm{kJ\,mol^{-1}\,K^{-1}}\).

Linearization for slope hunting

Eq.3.2.2 \( \ln k = -\frac{E_a}{R}\,\frac{1}{T} + \ln A \). Plot \(\ln k\) vs \(1/T\): slope \(m=-E_a/R\); intercept \(b=\ln A\).

Two-point shortcut

Eq.3.2.3 \( \ln(k_2/k_1)= -(E_a/R)\,[(1/T_2)-(1/T_1)] \).

Base-10 variant

Eq.3.2.4 \( \log_{10} k = -(E_a/2.303R)\,(1/T)+\log_{10}A \). Do not mix logs; forgetting 2.303 breaks your slope.

Modified Arrhenius (empirical)

Eq.3.2.5 \( k=A\,T^{n}\,e^{-E_a/(RT)} \). The exponent \(n\) can mop up degeneracy/entropic effects when a simple constant \(A\) misses curvature.

Plot hygiene. Use Kelvin; plot \(x=1/T\) in K\(^{-1}\). Slope units are K; multiply by \(R\) to get energy. Curvature across a wide \(T\) range often means a mechanism change or mixed regimes.

3.3 Transition State Theory (Eyring) and linkage to Arrhenius

Eq.3.3.1 Eyring equation: \[ k = \frac{k_B T}{h}\, e^{\Delta S^\ddagger/R}\, e^{-\Delta H^\ddagger/(RT)} \]

Eq.3.3.2 Gibbs activation form: \[ k = \frac{k_B T}{h}\, e^{-\Delta G^\ddagger/(RT)} \quad;\quad \Delta G^\ddagger=\Delta H^\ddagger - T\Delta S^\ddagger \]

Eq.3.3.3 Link to Arrhenius: \( E_a = \Delta H^\ddagger + RT \) for an elementary step under a fixed standard state.

Forward vs reverse barriers: Eq.3.3.4 \( E_{a,f}-E_{a,r}\approx \Delta H_{\mathrm{rxn}} \).

Equilibrium from rate constants: Eq.3.3.5 \( K \approx (A_f/A_r)\,e^{-(E_{a,f}-E_{a,r})/(RT)} \). Thermo insists \(K=e^{-\Delta G^\circ/(RT)}\); the ratio \(A_f/A_r\) embeds \(\Delta S^\circ\) and standard-state details.

3.4 Practical extraction recipes

Slope method (best with ≥4 temperatures)

  1. Compute \(\ln k\) at each \(T\).
  2. Regress \(\ln k\) vs \(1/T\) to get \(m,b\).
  3. Use Eq.3.2.2: \(E_a=-mR\) (report kJ mol\(^{-1}\)).
  4. \(A=e^b\) with units matching your reaction order.
  5. Predict \(k\) at a third \(T\) for validation.

Two-point method (exam classic, numerically twitchy)

  1. Choose \(T_1
  2. Eq.3.2.3 → \(E_a\).
  3. Back-solve \(A\) via Eq.3.2.1 at either \(T\).
  4. Sanity check against a third data point.

Eyring extraction of \(\Delta H^\ddagger,\Delta S^\ddagger\)

  1. Plot \(\ln(k/T)\) vs \(1/T\).
  2. Slope \(=-\Delta H^\ddagger/R\); intercept \(=\ln(k_B/h)+\Delta S^\ddagger/R\).
  3. \(\Delta G^\ddagger=\Delta H^\ddagger-T\Delta S^\ddagger\) at a chosen \(T\).
  4. Cross-check with Eq.3.3.3: \(E_a\stackrel{?}{\approx}\Delta H^\ddagger+RT\).

3.5 Interpreting \(A\) and \(E_a\) without mythology

  • Large \(E_a\) → strong \(T\)-sensitivity. Small \(T\) changes swing \(k\) hard.
  • \(A\) bundles frequency, orientation, and in TST the \(k_BT/h\) factor plus activation entropy.
  • \(\Delta S^\ddagger>0\): looser TS → effectively larger \(A\). \(\Delta S^\ddagger<0\): ordered TS → smaller \(A\).
  • Implausible \(A\) (e.g., \(10^{45}\ \mathrm{M^{-1}s^{-1}}\) for solution bimolecular) signals wrong mechanism or mixed regimes.

3.6 Dimensional and sign checks you must not botch

  • In Eq.3.2.2, slope has units of K; multiply by \(R\) to get energy.
  • Negative fitted \(E_a\)? Possible for diffusion-limited or complex pre-exponential \(T\)-dependence; often indicates mechanism changes or bad log handling.
  • Intercept gives \(A\) in the same units as \(k\); match to reaction order.

3.7 Worked-example blueprints (no numbers)

  1. From \(k(T)\) table → \(E_a\) and \(A\): slope/intercept mapping, predict a third-point \(k\).
  2. Two-point trap with base-10 logs: mind the 2.303 factor.
  3. Eyring: \(\ln(k/T)\) vs \(1/T\) → \(\Delta H^\ddagger,\Delta S^\ddagger\) and \(\Delta G^\ddagger\) at chosen \(T\).

3.8 Micro-review

  • Straight line of \(\ln k\) vs \(1/T\) → slope gives \(E_a\), intercept gives \(A\).
  • Eyring separates size of barrier \((\Delta H^\ddagger)\) from organization cost \((\Delta S^\ddagger)\).
  • For a simple elementary step: \(E_a\approx \Delta H^\ddagger+RT\); \(E_{a,f}-E_{a,r}\approx \Delta H_{rxn}\).
